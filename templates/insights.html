Editorial Briefing

Monsoon Drifts North Early, Redefining April Heat Stories

This week's pressure shifts reveal a gentler transition than seasonal models projected. While inland humidity rose in short bursts, the thermal stress curve remained below long-range median bands.

For planners, the most meaningful change is timing: rain corridors are not stronger, but earlier and more persistent through mornings. That subtle shift rebalances commute risk, crop irrigation windows, and construction pacing.

The current signal also hints at lower evening heat retention, particularly in mixed green-density zones. If this profile continues, cooling-energy demand will flatten in the next two weeks.
